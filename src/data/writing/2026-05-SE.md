Vi kan lære mye av gårsdagens telefoner. Mange av egenskapene jeg savner i dagens modeller finnes i eldre enheter som min iPhone SE (2016).

---

## Størrelse

4 inches. Fits in any pocket, one-handed everything, no repositioning your grip. The 16 Pro requires two hands or a death grip to type. This is the core argument — everything else is supporting evidence.

## Hodetelefonkontakten / The headphone jack

Classic but still valid. The SE has one; you always have earphones available. No dongles, no Bluetooth pairing dance, no dead battery on your AirPods mid-commute.

## Hjemknapp / Home button

Touch ID is instant muscle memory. Face ID requires you to look at the phone — it demands your attention. The SE lets you unlock without picking it up properly.

## Distraksjon / Distraction

The worse phone is the better phone. A small screen, no Dynamic Island, no ProMotion display trying to pull your eyes in — you do what you came to do and put it down. The 16 Pro is engineered to hold your attention.

## Kameraet er godt nok / The camera is fine

For everyday shots, the SE delivers. The 16 Pro's camera system is so capable it turns a quick snapshot into a decision: which lens? which mode? The SE removes the paralysis.

## Det jeg savner / What I miss

Be honest: battery life is genuinely worse, the small screen makes maps harder, and some apps no longer support iOS 15. Acknowledging the tradeoffs makes the preference more credible.

## Konklusjon / Conclusion

Not a hot take — just a different set of values. Less surface area, fewer features, more presence.
